# Ant Species Guide

## Which Ants Work With OpenFormicaria?

OpenFormicaria is designed for **medium to large ant species** — workers of roughly 3mm or larger. The 3mm-deep glass channel and FDM-printed surfaces are well-suited to these sizes, but very small ants can exploit tiny gaps.

**Safe:** Workers 3mm or larger — the vast majority of commonly kept species fall here.

**Borderline (extra sealing required):** Workers 2–3mm — doable with silicone sealing along the glass channel and Fluon in the outworld.

**Not recommended:** Workers under 2mm (ghost ants, thief ants, small Pheidole) — these can slip through gaps at the glass panel edge or around connector faces.

> **Tip:** Apply a thin bead of aquarium-safe silicone along the inner edge of the glass channel before seating the plexiglass panel. This closes any micro-gap from print artifacts or slight warping and is good practice regardless of species.

## Recommended Species for Beginners

### Lasius niger — Black Garden Ant *(best beginner choice in Europe)*

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Worker size | 3–5 mm |
| Temperature | 20–25°C nest; hibernate 5–10°C (Oct–Mar) |
| Humidity | 50–60% nest, 30–50% outworld |
| Escape risk | Low |
| Formicarium suitability | Excellent — shallow disc-shaped chambers suit the module design perfectly |

Easy to find, forgiving of care mistakes, and very active. Widely recommended across the ant-keeping community as the best first species for 3D printed modular setups.

---

### Camponotus — Carpenter Ants *(best for larger setups)*

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Worker size | 6–14 mm (polymorphic) |
| Temperature | 21–26°C; hibernate 10–15°C (Oct–Mar) for European species |
| Humidity | 50–70% |
| Escape risk | Very low — large workers eliminate gap-escape risk entirely |
| Formicarium suitability | Excellent |

Large, visible, slow to escape. The downside is slow colony growth — expect up to a year before the colony is large enough to move from a test tube to the formicarium.

---

### Formica — Wood Ants / Field Ants

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Worker size | 5–8 mm |
| Temperature | 20–28°C; requires winter hibernation |
| Humidity | 40–70% |
| Escape risk | **High** — extremely fast; tight enclosures essential |
| Formicarium suitability | Good, with secure lid and Fluon in outworld |

---

### Tetramorium caespitum — Pavement Ant *(borderline size)*

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Worker size | 2.5–4 mm |
| Temperature | 21–26°C |
| Humidity | 50–60% |
| Escape risk | **High for their size** — known for finding hairline gaps |
| Formicarium suitability | Possible, but seal the glass channel with silicone and apply Fluon |

---

### Messor barbarus — Harvester Ant *(dry-climate species)*

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Worker size | 3–8 mm (polymorphic) |
| Temperature | 24–28°C |
| Humidity | Low — dry nest, slightly moister area for brood |
| Escape risk | Low |
| Formicarium suitability | Good — use the dry side of a moisture-gradient setup |

Popular in Southern Europe. Seed-harvesting behaviour makes them interesting to observe.

---

## Chamber Depth: Is 15mm Deep Enough?

Yes — for all species listed above. Natural ant chambers are flat and disc-shaped, rarely more than a few centimetres deep. The 15mm ant cutout in a 20mm module closely mirrors how ants nest in the wild. Queens of large Camponotus species (16–20mm body length) cannot stand fully upright, but adopt a prostrate posture in chambers naturally — this is not a problem.
